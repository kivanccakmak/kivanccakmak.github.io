Law of Large Numbers

This post aims to explain Law of Large numbers. To do so, we first introduce Chebyshev Inequality, then provide the theorem and proof of the law of large numbers.

Chebyshev Inequality:

Theorem:
Let X be a random variable with mean E[X] = \mu and variance \mathrm{Var}[X] = \sigma^2.

Then, the Chebyshev inequality states that

P(|X - \mu| \geq t) \leq \frac{\sigma^2}{t^2}, for t > 0.

Proof:
Let A denote the event |X - \mu| \geq t, and A^{c} the complementary event (|X - \mu| < \mu )

\sigma^2 = E[(X - \mu)|A]P(A) + E[(X - \mu)|A^{c}]P(A^{c})

\sigma^2 \geq E[(X - \mu)|A]P(A)

since 0 \leq P(A^{c}) \leq 1 and E[(X - \mu)|A^{c}] \geq 0.

So, whenever A occurs, |X - \mu| \geq t, which implies (X - \mu) \geq t^{2}. Here, we can say that: E[(X - \mu)^2 | A] \geq t^{2}

finally, by using Equation 3, can say that

\sigma^2 \geq t^2 P(A), which alternatively means: P(|X - \mu| \geq t) \leq \frac{\sigma^2}{t^2}
